hello! my name is liang.

i'm a master's student studying theoretical neuroscience at ucl's gatsby unit. i did my undergrad at mit, studying bcs and eecs and working in the cocosci lab. non-academically, i've been part of eecscon, movementality, dancetroupe, adt, dynamit, savetfp and makemit.

i'm from sunny socal, i can fold origami dragons, and i enjoy running and hip-hop dancing in my free time.

to come!

i'm fascinated by how people think. as individuals, we act, feel, imagine, and live in a way that defines the essence of being human. in particular, i'm interested in the neural underpinnings of higher-level cognitive activities, such as biased decision-making and social pressure.

research i've been a part of:

  1. tower counterfactuals

    2. paper visualization the goal is to model people's counterfactual judgments of stability by generating noisy simulations of brick towers and designating a particular "special" brick to remove. we ran several amazon mturk experiments to sample human judgment and created a noisy simulation model that better represented people's intuitive judgments.

  2. 3d micropillar modelling

    3. writeup i created a java processing suite to model the geometry of 3d-printed high-res micropillar structures, which have a collective ability to bend and represent a vector field in their direction of orientation. using a prepared image as input, we can output a representation of the resultant hair structure based on image gradient properties.
